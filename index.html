Platform

Ben will achieve equity and environmental protection in the Bay Area by making buses fares more affordable, improving transfers between AC Transit and the BART, expanding dedicated bus lanes, and pushing for more environmentally friendly buses.

  • Equity
    • Cheaper bus transfers between AC Transit and BART
    • Cheaper monthly pass option and provide a $5 unlimited daily pass option
    • Better coordination with city government
    • Hiring of previously incarcerated individuals
  • Convenience
    • Electronic signs at bus stops with upcoming bus arrivals
    • Improve bus efficiencies by coordinating traffic signals during commute hours
    • Provide better service to BART stations and ferry terminals for last mile
  • Environmentally friendly
    • Accelerate timetable to get electric and hydrogen powered buses to meet climate goals
    • Evaluate bus routes on how to best reduce pollution and also reduce pollution for underserved communities

About

Ben Fong is a transportation advocate with years of experience promoting sustainable and affordable transportation across the US through his work bringing bikeshare and scooter share programs across over 100 cities. Ben has been a policy advocate his entire life - including as a long-time Berkeley Planning Commissioner and as a former official in the Obama Administration where he served as a U.S. diplomat and Foreign Service Officer with tours in Mexico, India, and DC.

Ben’s connections to the East Bay go back several generations to the early 1900s. Ben’s grandmother went to Oakland High School and his great grandparents are buried in Mountain View Cemetery in Oakland. Ben is a community advocate and is the founder of A Fork in the Road a community initiative supporting local restaurants transition to cafe seating to help struggling small and minority-owned businesses during the Covid crisis. As a Berkeley Planning Commissioner, he made it easier to build affordable housing, including through streamlining Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs), securing affordable housing units at buildings such as 2747 San Pablo, and reducing parking requirements for buildings near public transit hubs.

After completing his Master in Public Affairs from Princeton’s School of Policy and International Affairs, Ben was inspired to serve his country. Ben was commissioned by a Foreign Service Officer by Secretary of State Hillary Clinton. As an official in the Obama Administration, Ben represented the United States as a person of color where he promoted American democracy, diversity, and human rights. Ben served his country including tours in Mexico where he worked on immigration and trade issues on the U.S.-Mexico border, in India, and in Washington DC. Ben speaks Spanish, Hindi, Mandarin and English.
